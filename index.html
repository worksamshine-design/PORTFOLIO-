Project 01 · Branding · Product Design

Waou —
Bespoke Dog Accessory Brand

A luxury bespoke brand designed for dogs and their owners, blending emotional safety with high design and functionality.

The Brief

This project started from exploring brands and manufacturing types, coming across the term bespoke for the first time. The mission was to create an emotional safety net for dogs while easing their owners' tasks — designing for the dog but keeping the owner's needs and comfort in mind.

Products Designed

  • Petal Hause — Converts into a car seat, small carry bag, flattens as a bed. Travel friendly with essential oil drops for familiarity, calm and peacefulness.
  • Digi Tail — Gives syntactic breathing feature mimicking human heartbeat, plays music, gives instructions, prevents tangling.
  • Rolla Polla — Scientifically designed textures to reduce anxiety, depends on dog's weight, easy to clean.
  • WalkMate — Has everything owner and dog need while walking — trash bag dispenser, water bowl, data app, ergonomic handle.

Project 03 · UX · Product Design

Kitsy First Aid Kits —
Redefining Emergency Readiness

A kid-friendly first aid kit designed for clarity, speed, and guided confidence in emergencies.

Problem Statement

Kids are unable to use basic first aid on their own without someone present to help them — resulting in small reactions turning worse, kid panicking, worried parents, and situations where wrong or expired medication causes greater problems than the original issue.

Key Insight

Users don't need more tools — they need clarity, speed, and guidance during stressful emergency situations. 97% of people during research interviews said this is a major gap and they would buy anything that helps them keep their children safe.

Design Guidelines

  • Kids want colourful options and different shapes to find the kit interesting.
  • Should be colourful in like soothing shades to eliminate fear — not medical white and red.
  • Should feel like a game or a toy, but not be abandoned — maybe a bit cute.
  • Smart App Connectivity with AR/QR tutorials and automated inventory alerts.

Project 04 · Research Paper · Inclusive Design

Sitting & Spacing Issues —
Elderly People on Local Trains

A research paper identifying spatial and accessibility gaps faced by elderly passengers on local trains, proposing human-centered design interventions.

Why This Topic?

Millions commute daily, but elderly passengers face severe accessibility gaps — high steps, lack of ergonomic seating, and overcrowded aisles create massive physical and emotional barriers. With an aging population, improving transport for elderly people can enhance their independence and quality of life.

This project involved 6 weeks of research through observations, interviews, surveys, and talking to railway workers.

Proposed Ideas

  • L-Shaped Seating — Redesigned seating incorporating individual armrests to provide necessary leverage for standing up and significantly better lumbar support.
  • Extended Handrails — Lowered handlebar extensions strategically placed to offer crucial grip and stability while the train is in rapid motion.
  • Accessible Storage — Lowered and repositioned luggage racks to ensure elderly passengers can easily stow and retrieve personal belongings without physical strain.
  • Lowered Handlebars — Low height hand bars at the top in the spaces between seats for standing.

Project 05 · HCD Research · Product Design

The Recovery Paradox
& Lego Zen

An end-to-end HCD case study translating comprehensive post-workout qualitative research into a modular hot & cold field massager.

Introduction

This project addresses a critical contradiction in modern fitness — the Recovery Paradox. While biologically muscle growth and adaptation occur during rest, not during the workout itself, recovery is consistently the first activity people neglect when life gets busy.

Through an exploratory Human Centered Design framework, we investigated the deep behavioral and environmental disconnects driving this failure — discovering it's not simply a lack of motivation but a systemic failure where Contextual Friction prevents users from maximizing their fitness potential.

Lego Zen — The Product

  • Magnetic Connections — High-strength neodymium magnets to self-align, enabling intuitive setup based on specific field injury regions.
  • Active Thermal Pulse — Swappable cooling/heating compression patches with precise electronic temperatures.
  • Adaptive Strapping — Fastening elastics and durable velcro straps anchor the module to leg, arm, joints, back or neck — hands-free.
